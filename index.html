How real retirees spend

Your spending won't be a flat line

Most planning assumes one spending number stretched across 30 years. In reality, retirement moves through three distinct phases — and knowing which one you're in changes the math.

✈️
Go-Go · ~62–74

Go-Go Years

The active early years: travel, hobbies, helping the kids, the things you retired for. Discretionary spending is at its highest — and a realistic plan should expect that, not assume you'll live lean from day one.

🌿
Slow-Go · ~75–84

Slow-Go Years

The pace eases. Less long-haul travel, more time close to home. Spending typically settles 20–30% below the Go-Go years — which can meaningfully extend how long a portfolio lasts.

🏡
No-Go · 85+

No-Go Years

Activity slows further, but healthcare and potential long-term-care costs can rise. Discretionary spending falls while medical costs climb — two curves moving in opposite directions.

Treating retirement as three phases instead of one flat number usually changes the answer — often for the better, since the expensive years come first and the frugal years last longer than people expect. The Spending Phases Planner lets you put your own numbers against each phase.
